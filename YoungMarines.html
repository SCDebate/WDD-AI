About Us

The Young Marines is a youth education and service program for boys and girls, ages 8 through the completion of high school. The program promotes the mental, moral, and physical development of its members. The Young Marines is the focal point for the Marine Corps' Youth Drug Demand Reduction efforts.

Our program emphasizes the importance of teamwork, leadership, and community service. Members participate in a variety of activities designed to build character and instill values that will serve them throughout their lives.

Our Mission

The mission of the Young Marines is to positively impact America's future by providing quality youth development programs for boys and girls that nurture and develop its members into responsible citizens who enjoy and promote a healthy, drug-free lifestyle.

We aim to instill a sense of pride and responsibility in our members, encouraging them to become active and engaged citizens. Our programs are designed to challenge and inspire young people, helping them to reach their full potential.

Programs

The Young Marines offers a variety of programs and activities, including leadership schools, encampments, and community service projects. These programs are designed to instill the core values of leadership, teamwork, and discipline in its members.

Our programs include:

  • Leadership Training: Courses and workshops that focus on developing leadership skills and qualities.
  • Physical Fitness: Activities and challenges that promote physical health and well-being.
  • Community Service: Projects that encourage members to give back to their communities.
  • Educational Workshops: Sessions on topics such as first aid, public speaking, and financial literacy.

Join Us

If you are interested in joining the Young Marines or would like more information, please visit our website or contact your local unit. We welcome all youth who are looking for a challenging and rewarding experience.

Joining the Young Marines provides an opportunity to develop new skills, make lifelong friends, and become part of a supportive and motivated community. Our members are encouraged to set goals, work hard, and strive for excellence in all they do.

History

The Young Marines was founded in 1959 and has grown to become a national organization with units in nearly every state. Over the years, the program has evolved to meet the changing needs of youth, but its core values and mission have remained the same.

Our history is rich with stories of personal growth, community impact, and national recognition. From our humble beginnings to our current status as a leading youth development organization, the Young Marines has always been dedicated to fostering the leaders of tomorrow.

Leadership

The Young Marines is led by a dedicated team of volunteers, many of whom are former Marines or have military backgrounds. These leaders are committed to providing a positive and supportive environment for all members.

Our leadership team works tirelessly to ensure that our programs are effective, engaging, and accessible to all youth. They bring a wealth of experience and knowledge to the organization, helping to guide and mentor our members as they grow and develop.

Community Service

Community service is a key component of the Young Marines program. Members participate in a wide range of service projects, from local clean-up efforts to national campaigns. These activities help instill a sense of civic responsibility and pride in their communities.

Our community service projects include:

  • Environmental Clean-Ups: Organizing and participating in efforts to clean and preserve local parks, beaches, and other natural areas.
  • Veteran Support: Assisting veterans through various initiatives, such as visiting VA hospitals and participating in memorial services.
  • Charity Drives: Collecting and distributing food, clothing, and other necessities to those in need.
  • Educational Outreach: Providing tutoring and mentoring services to younger students in the community.

Testimonials

Many former Young Marines have gone on to successful careers in the military, law enforcement, and other fields. Their experiences in the program have helped shape their character and provided them with valuable skills and experiences.

Our alumni often share their stories of how the Young Marines program has positively impacted their lives. These testimonials highlight the lasting benefits of the program and the strong sense of community and support that it fosters.

Training and Education

The Young Marines program includes a comprehensive training and education component. Members learn about military customs and courtesies, physical fitness, first aid, and more. The program also emphasizes the importance of academic achievement and encourages members to excel in school.

Our training and education initiatives include:

  • Military Skills: Instruction on drill and ceremony, uniform regulations, and other military traditions.
  • Health and Wellness: Programs that promote physical fitness, nutrition, and mental health awareness.
  • Academic Support: Tutoring and study sessions to help members achieve their educational goals.
  • Life Skills: Workshops on topics such as financial literacy, time management, and career planning.

Leadership Schools

The Young Marines offers several leadership schools that provide advanced training for members who demonstrate exceptional leadership potential. These schools focus on developing skills such as public speaking, team building, and problem-solving.

Our leadership schools are designed to challenge and inspire our members, helping them to develop the confidence and skills needed to lead effectively. These schools include:

  • Junior Leadership School: An introductory program for younger members that focuses on basic leadership principles and skills.
  • Senior Leadership School: An advanced program for older members that includes more complex leadership challenges and scenarios.
  • National Leadership Academy: A prestigious program that brings together top Young Marines from across the country for intensive leadership training and development.

Encampments

Encampments are a highlight of the Young Marines program. These events provide members with the opportunity to participate in outdoor activities, develop survival skills, and build camaraderie with their peers. Encampments are held at various locations across the country and offer a unique and challenging experience.

Our encampments include a variety of activities, such as:

  • Camping: Setting up and maintaining a campsite, cooking meals, and learning outdoor survival skills.
  • Hiking: Exploring nature trails and learning about the local environment and wildlife.
  • Team Challenges: Participating in obstacle courses, team-building exercises, and other group activities.
  • Workshops: Attending sessions on topics such as navigation, first aid, and environmental conservation.

Drug Demand Reduction

The Young Marines is committed to promoting a drug-free lifestyle. The program includes a strong drug demand reduction component that educates members about the dangers of drug use and provides them with the tools they need to make healthy choices. Members also participate in community outreach efforts to spread the message of drug prevention.

Our drug demand reduction initiatives include:

  • Educational Workshops: Sessions that provide information on the dangers of drug use and strategies for making healthy choices.
  • Peer Mentoring: Programs that encourage older members to mentor younger members and promote a drug-free lifestyle.
  • Community Outreach: Efforts to raise awareness about drug prevention through events, campaigns, and partnerships with local organizations.
  • Support Services: Access to resources and support for members who may be struggling with substance abuse issues.

Recognition and Awards

The Young Marines program includes a variety of recognition and awards to motivate and reward members for their achievements. These include ribbons, medals, and certificates for accomplishments in areas such as leadership, physical fitness, and community service.

Our recognition and awards program includes:

  • Achievement Ribbons: Awards for completing specific challenges and milestones within the program.
  • Medals of Honor: Special recognition for outstanding leadership, bravery, and service.
  • Certificates of Achievement: Formal recognition of accomplishments in areas such as academics, physical fitness, and community service.
  • Annual Awards Ceremony: An event that celebrates the achievements of our members and recognizes their hard work and dedication.

Partnerships

The Young Marines works closely with a variety of organizations to enhance its programs and provide additional opportunities for its members. These partnerships include collaborations with military units, law enforcement agencies, and community organizations.

Our partnerships help to expand the reach and impact of our programs, providing valuable resources and support. These partnerships include:

  • Military Collaborations: Working with local military units to provide training, resources, and support for our members.
  • Law Enforcement Partnerships: Collaborating with police departments and other law enforcement agencies to promote safety and crime prevention.
  • Community Organizations: Partnering with local nonprofits, schools, and other organizations to support community service projects and educational initiatives.

Alumni

The Young Marines has a strong network of alumni who continue to support the program and its mission. Many alumni return to serve as volunteers and mentors, helping to guide the next generation of Young Marines. The program also offers opportunities for alumni to stay connected through events and reunions.

Our alumni network provides a valuable resource for current members, offering mentorship, support, and opportunities for personal and professional growth. Alumni initiatives include:
