About Us

The Young Marines is a youth education and service program for boys and girls, ages 8 through the completion of high school. The program promotes the mental, moral, and physical development of its members. The Young Marines is the focal point for the Marine Corps' Youth Drug Demand Reduction efforts.

Our program emphasizes the importance of teamwork, leadership, and community service. Members participate in a variety of activities designed to build character and instill values that will serve them throughout their lives.

Our Mission

The mission of the Young Marines is to positively impact America's future by providing quality youth development programs for boys and girls that nurture and develop its members into responsible citizens who enjoy and promote a healthy, drug-free lifestyle.

We aim to instill a sense of pride and responsibility in our members, encouraging them to become active and engaged citizens. Our programs are designed to challenge and inspire young people, helping them to reach their full potential.

Programs

The Young Marines offers a variety of programs and activities, including leadership schools, encampments, and community service projects. These programs are designed to instill the core values of leadership, teamwork, and discipline in its members.

Our programs include:

  • Leadership Training: Courses and workshops that focus on developing leadership skills and qualities.
  • Physical Fitness: Activities and challenges that promote physical health and well-being.
  • Community Service: Projects that encourage members to give back to their communities.
  • Educational Workshops: Sessions on topics such as first aid, public speaking, and financial literacy.

Leadership Schools

The Young Marines offers several leadership schools that provide advanced training for members who demonstrate exceptional leadership potential. These schools focus on developing skills such as public speaking, team building, and problem-solving.

Our leadership schools are designed to challenge and inspire our members, helping them to develop the confidence and skills needed to lead effectively. These schools include:

  • Junior Leadership School: An introductory program for younger members that focuses on basic leadership principles and skills.
  • Senior Leadership School: An advanced program for older members that includes more complex leadership challenges and scenarios.
  • National Leadership Academy: A prestigious program that brings together top Young Marines from across the country for intensive leadership training and development.

Encampments

Our encampments provide members with the opportunity to participate in immersive training experiences. These events are designed to simulate real-world scenarios and challenges, allowing members to apply their skills and knowledge in a practical setting.

Encampments include activities such as survival training, team building exercises, and leadership challenges. They are a valuable opportunity for members to develop their skills, build camaraderie, and have fun.

Join Us

If you're interested in joining the Young Marines, we welcome boys and girls between the ages of 8 and high school completion. To become a member, you can visit our website and find a local unit near you. Each unit has its own enrollment process, so be sure to reach out to them for more information.

Joining the Young Marines is a great opportunity to develop leadership skills, participate in community service, and make lifelong friends. We look forward to welcoming you to our program!

History

The Young Marines was founded in 1958 and has since grown into one of the nation's premier youth organizations. Our program has a rich history of providing young people with opportunities for personal growth, leadership development, and community service.

Over the years, the Young Marines has received numerous awards and recognition for its impact on youth development and drug demand reduction efforts. We are proud of our history and the positive impact we continue to make in the lives of young people.

Leadership

The Young Marines is led by a dedicated team of adult volunteers who are committed to the development and success of our members. Our leadership team includes experienced professionals from various backgrounds who bring their expertise and passion to our program.

Our leaders undergo training and certification to ensure they provide a safe and supportive environment for our members. They serve as mentors, role models, and guides, helping our young Marines reach their full potential.

Community Service

Community service is a core component of the Young Marines program. We believe in the importance of giving back to our communities and making a positive impact. Our members participate in a wide range of community service projects, including environmental cleanups, food drives, and volunteering at local organizations.

Through community service, our members learn the value of helping others and develop a sense of responsibility and empathy. It is an integral part of our program and helps shape our members into responsible and compassionate citizens.

Training and Education

The Young Marines provides comprehensive training and education to its members. Our program includes a variety of courses and workshops that cover topics such as leadership, first aid, navigation, and more.

Members have the opportunity to earn certifications and badges as they progress through the program. These achievements recognize their dedication and mastery of specific skills. Our training and education program is designed to prepare our members for success in all aspects of life.

Drug Demand Reduction

As the focal point for the Marine Corps' Youth Drug Demand Reduction efforts, the Young Marines is committed to promoting a healthy, drug-free lifestyle. We educate our members about the dangers of drugs and provide them with the tools and support they need to make positive choices.

Through our drug demand reduction program, we aim to empower our members to resist peer pressure and make informed decisions. We believe in the power of prevention and early intervention in addressing drug-related issues.
