Young Marines

About Us

The Young Marines is a youth education and service program for boys and girls, ages 8 through the completion of high school. The program promotes the mental, moral, and physical development of its members. The Young Marines is the focal point for the Marine Corps' Youth Drug Demand Reduction efforts.

Our Mission

The mission of the Young Marines is to positively impact America's future by providing quality youth development programs for boys and girls that nurtures and develops its members into responsible citizens who enjoy and promote a healthy, drug-free lifestyle.

Programs

The Young Marines offers a variety of programs and activities, including leadership schools, encampments, and community service projects. These programs are designed to instill the core values of leadership, teamwork, and discipline in its members.

Join Us

If you are interested in joining the Young Marines or would like more information, please visit our website or contact your local unit. We welcome all youth who are looking for a challenging and rewarding experience.

History

The Young Marines was founded in 1959 and has grown to become a national organization with units in nearly every state. Over the years, the program has evolved to meet the changing needs of youth, but its core values and mission have remained the same.

Leadership

The Young Marines is led by a dedicated team of volunteers, many of whom are former Marines or have military backgrounds. These leaders are committed to providing a positive and supportive environment for all members.

Community Service

Community service is a key component of the Young Marines program. Members participate in a wide range of service projects, from local clean-up efforts to national campaigns. These activities help instill a sense of civic responsibility and pride in their communities.

Testimonials

Many former Young Marines have gone on to successful careers in the military, law enforcement, and other fields. Their experiences in the program have helped shape their character and provided them with valuable skills and experiences.
